How to Clean an Air Fryer: Complete Step-by-Step Guide

Cleaning your air fryer might not be the most exciting part of cooking, but it's one of the most important. A clean air fryer cooks food more evenly, lasts longer, and helps prevent unwanted smoke and odors.

Many beginners make the mistake of leaving grease and food residue inside the basket after every meal. Over time, this buildup can affect both the taste of your food and the performance of your appliance.

In this guide, you'll learn how to clean your air fryer safely, what cleaning products to use, and common mistakes to avoid.

Why cleaning your air fryer matters

Cleaning your air fryer regularly helps you:

  • Prevent grease buildup
  • Remove unpleasant cooking odors
  • Improve cooking performance
  • Extend the lifespan of your appliance
  • Keep food tasting fresh and delicious

A clean air fryer isn't just about appearance—it's essential for better cooking results and long-term performance.

What you'll need to clean your air fryer

Supplies to gather:

  • Warm water
  • Mild dish soap
  • Soft sponge
  • Microfiber cloth
  • Soft-bristle brush
  • Baking soda (for stubborn stains)

Avoid using:

  • Steel wool
  • Metal scrubbers
  • Harsh chemical cleaners
  • Abrasive scouring pads

These materials can damage the non-stick coating and reduce your air fryer's performance.

Step-by-step guide to cleaning your air fryer

Step 1: Unplug and cool down

Always unplug your air fryer and allow it to cool completely before cleaning. Never clean the appliance while it's still hot. This prevents burns and allows grease to solidify, making it easier to remove.

Step 2: Remove the basket and tray

Take out the basket and crisper tray. Wash them using warm water, mild dish soap, and a soft sponge. If your model has dishwasher-safe parts, check the manufacturer's instructions before placing them in the dishwasher. Most modern air fryer baskets are dishwasher-safe.

Step 3: Clean the inside

Use a damp microfiber cloth to wipe the interior of the cooking chamber. For stuck-on grease, gently scrub with a soft brush. Avoid pouring water directly into the appliance to prevent electrical damage.

Step 4: Clean the heating element

Turn the air fryer upside down carefully. Use a soft brush or cloth to remove food particles from the heating element. This area is often overlooked but can collect grease over time, affecting performance.

Step 5: Wipe the exterior

Clean the outside of the air fryer using a damp cloth. Dry it with a clean microfiber towel before storing it. This prevents water damage and keeps your appliance looking new.

How to remove tough grease and stains

For stubborn grease stains that won't come off easily:

  1. Mix baking soda with a small amount of water to form a paste
  2. Apply it to the stained area
  3. Let it sit for 10–15 minutes
  4. Wipe gently with a soft sponge
  5. Rinse and dry thoroughly

Important: Avoid soaking electrical components in water. Only clean removable parts this way.

Air fryer cleaning schedule

Air Fryer Part Cleaning Frequency
Basket After every use
Crisper Tray After every use
Interior Weekly
Heating Element Every 2–4 weeks
Exterior Weekly

Common cleaning mistakes to avoid

  • Cleaning while hot: Always wait for the air fryer to cool completely
  • Using metal scrubbers: They can damage the non-stick coating permanently
  • Soaking the main unit: Never submerge electrical components in water
  • Forgetting the heating element: This area collects grease and affects performance
  • Leaving grease buildup: Don't let residue accumulate over multiple cooking sessions

Maintenance tips for long-term performance

To keep your air fryer in excellent condition for years:

  • Clean the basket after every meal without exception
  • Empty food crumbs regularly to prevent burning
  • Avoid using cooking sprays that can damage some non-stick coatings
  • Dry all removable parts completely before reassembling
  • Store the appliance in a dry place away from moisture

Frequently asked questions

Can I put my air fryer basket in the dishwasher?
Many air fryer baskets are dishwasher-safe, but always check your user manual first. Different brands have different recommendations.

Can I use baking soda to clean an air fryer?
Yes. Baking soda is a safe and effective option for removing stubborn grease and stains without damaging the non-stick coating.

How often should I clean my air fryer?
The basket and tray should be cleaned after every use, while the interior and heating element should be cleaned regularly based on usage—weekly for interior, every 2–4 weeks for the heating element.

Why does my air fryer smell bad?
Food residue and grease buildup are common causes. Regular cleaning usually eliminates unpleasant odors. If the smell persists, it may indicate a malfunction.

Can I use metal scrubbers?
No. Metal scrubbers can scratch and damage the non-stick coating, reducing your air fryer's effectiveness and lifespan.

Final verdict

Keeping your air fryer clean is one of the easiest ways to improve its performance and extend its lifespan. By cleaning the basket after every use, wiping the interior regularly, and maintaining the heating element, you'll enjoy better-tasting food and a more reliable appliance.

If you're new to air frying, proper maintenance is just as important as choosing the right appliance and learning new recipes. A few minutes of cleaning after each meal can save you time and keep your air fryer working like new for years.
